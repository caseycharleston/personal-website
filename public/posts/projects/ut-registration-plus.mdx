# Overview

As part of UT Austin's Longhorn Developers organization, I developed an update with a group of developers to the UT Registration Plus chrome extension. You can see the extension on the chrome web store [here](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/ut-registration-plus/hboadpjkoaieogjimneceaahlppnipaa?hl=en). This chrome extension helps UT Austin students register for classes by providing an all-in-one UI solution to research and plan class schedules.

# What I worked on

![](posts/images/ut-registration-plus-feat.png)

In v1, the extension only allowed students to create a single schedule. In some cases, it's useful to have multiple schedules as it's not guaranteed that a student will be able to register for all of their desired classes.

A new feature in v2 allows users to create multiple schedules. I worked on the UI for multiple schedule handling. Any one schedule can be selected at a time, displaying the classes in that schedule to the right. They can also be reordered by dragging and dropping them in the "My Schedules" list in any order.

I also coded the actions in the dropdown menu for each schedule. The dropdown allows users to rename, duplicate, and delete a schedule. You can also rename a schedule by double clicking on the schedule name. My favorite feature is the duplicate. Many of my schedules are the exact same as a previous one with one class changed. Duplicating a schedule allows me to quickly change one class and have a new schedule ready to go.
