# What is Sugoroku?

> This project was built along with a team of programmers, designers, and historians as part of UT Austin's JapanLab. For more information, visit their [website](https://www.utjapanlab.com/). For the code associated with this project, see the GitHub repository [here](https://github.com/caseycharleston/Sugoroku).

Sugoroku is a Japanese dice-rollig board game. Each turn, a player rolls two dice (Sogoroku literally translates to "double six"). The number a player rolls determines how many spaces they can move on the board. Each space on the board has the potential to move the player forward or backwards, similar to Snakes and Ladders. The first player to reach the end of the board wins. For more info, see the Wikipedia page [here](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sugoroku).

What's so interesting about Sugoroku is its audience and subject matter. The game is primarily targeted towards children and the spaces on the board often depict Japanese history and culture. With this in mind, For example, look at the first tile in the header image:
![](posts/images/sugoroku-tile1.png)

The tile above shows two women adding stitches to cloth. This practice is known as _Senninbari_ which translates to "thousand person stitches." Traditionally, each stitch is made by a different woman and the completed cloth was given to soldiers as a good luck charm.

Each tile on the board has a similar story behind it, whether it be a historical event or a cultural practice.

## Developing the Game

Our team worked on creating a digital version of Sugoroku with Unity. The primary focus of the game is to educate players on the tiles of the board. Our version of the game has built-in text for each tile that explains what it represents in a Japanese historical context.

## Demoing the Game

After developing the game, we showed it off at the JapanLab Expo in Spring 2023. We showed the game off to other students an faculty at UT Austin.
