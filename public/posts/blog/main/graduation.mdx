# About jobs

If you are even 1% interested in software engineering, invest in stocks, or keep up with whatever is going on in Elon Musk's insane mind, you've probably seen that the job market for software engineers is ludicrously competitive and incredibly volatile. Tech companies are laying people off, even when profits are at all time highs. It’s a scary time to be a software engineer, especially a junior one like me. Honestly, I'm _incredibly_ lucky and grateful to have a job.

During my time as a UTCS Ambassador, the briefed us on some of stats about UTCS graduates that parents and prospective students will most likely ask about. The one stat that I immediately knew was out of date was the job placement rate. In 2019, this was above 90% -- an incredible number. Nowadays, I'd estimate that 60-70% of UTCS graduates are getting jobs right out of college. While my guess is that the majority are still getting jobs, this could easily drop or rise year by year. And that is the scariest part: the uncertainty. Being a CS major is no longer low risk, for a high reward. The risk is higher than ever.

## A side note/rant
